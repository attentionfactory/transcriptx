# SEO Strategy

## Positioning

**What we are:** the cheapest unlimited transcription tool that works on the most sites. $3.99/mo, 1000+ sources, paste-a-link flow.

**What we are not:** a meeting bot (that's Otter), a video editor (that's Descript), an enterprise compliance tool (that's Rev). Don't chase those keywords.

## Keyword tiers

### Tier A — head terms we must show up for
Already have landing pages; job is to keep improving them.

- `youtube transcript generator`
- `video to text`
- `audio to transcript`
- `transcribe video online`
- `free transcription tool`

### Tier B — comparison pages (high intent)
Already have most of these; job is to keep the comparison honest.

- `otter vs transcriptx` (and inverse)
- `descript vs transcriptx`
- `notta vs transcriptx`
- `rev vs transcriptx`
- `best youtube transcript tools`

Add:
- `tokscript vs transcriptx` (TikTok-specific competitor)
- `free otter alternative`
- `free descript alternative for transcription`

### Tier C — problem-specific guides
This is where the [content-guides-backlog](content-guides-backlog.md) lives. Each guide targets one exact Google query.

### Tier D — supported-site pages
The 1000+ platform support index. One page per major site, showing "yes we transcribe [site]". Currently generated; low effort, modest compounding traffic.

## What we stop doing

- **Generic "what is transcription" content.** Commodity, low intent, unrankable.
- **"Ultimate guide to X" link-bait.** We don't have the domain authority to rank for it, and it's the first thing users skip.
- **Keyword-stuffed landing pages.** The old `platform-support-index` was trending this way before the plain-English rewrite.

## Internal linking rules

- Every guide links to at least one supported-site page and the pricing page.
- Every supported-site page links to at least one relevant guide.
- Every comparison page links to `/pricing` and the most relevant tool page (e.g., `/youtube-transcript-generator` on a YouTube-centric comparison).
- Homepage keeps links tight — no 40-link footer dump.

## Measurement

- Monthly GSC review: impressions by query, top rising queries, top falling queries.
- Per-page CTR check: anything <1% at 500+ impressions → meta title/desc rewrite.
- Conversion: signup rate from SEO traffic, segmented by tier (A/B/C/D).

## Kill criteria for the SEO bet as a whole

- If 6 months from now organic signups <5% of total signups, SEO isn't the right acquisition channel for us at this stage and we should redirect effort to referrals and product-led growth.
