# Activation & Retention

## Funnel as it stands

1. Land on homepage or SEO page.
2. Paste a URL (no signup required? check current state).
3. Signup wall at transcript result / export.
4. First transcript delivered.
5. Come back next month / upgrade to Pro.

## Activation hypotheses

### 1. Instant transcript preview without signup
Show the first ~30 seconds transcribed before the signup wall.
- **Hypothesis:** proving the output quality up front converts higher than promising it.
- **Worked:** signup rate lifts ≥20%.
- **Failed:** rate drops or flat → we gave away the value for free.

### 2. "Your transcript is ready, email it to you" flow
If a user pastes a URL and bounces, email them when it's done.
- **Hypothesis:** recovers users who lost patience but still want the transcript.
- **Worked:** ≥5% of email-completions return and complete signup.
- **Failed:** low email capture or low return rate.

### 3. Sample transcript gallery
Homepage shows real transcripts of famous YouTube videos, podcasts, TED talks.
- **Hypothesis:** lets users see quality without trying themselves.
- **Worked:** lifts paste-URL rate.
- **Failed:** no shift → trust isn't the bottleneck.

## Retention hypotheses

### 4. Monthly reset reminder email
Day-before-reset email: "Your 3 free transcripts reset tomorrow — here are 3 videos worth transcribing."
- **Hypothesis:** reactivates dormant free users.
- **Worked:** open rate >30%, reactivation ≥5%.
- **Failed:** <15% open or 0 measurable reactivation.

### 5. Transcript history dashboard
Keep a user's past transcripts searchable.
- **Hypothesis:** makes us a notebook, not a one-shot tool. Users come back.
- **Worked:** weekly active users ≥1.5× current.
- **Failed:** users still come once/month only.

### 6. Export format expansion
SRT, VTT, DOCX, Notion import, Google Docs direct.
- **Hypothesis:** specific export formats keep users in-product instead of bouncing to a better export.
- **Worked:** export-feature usage ≥20% of transcripts, retention lift on users who use them.
- **Failed:** <5% usage of each new format.

## Aha-moment definition

First paid conversion happens when a user has:
- Finished ≥2 transcripts
- Exported at least one
- Come back on a different day

This is what the activation metrics should optimize for.

## Kill criteria

- If day-7 retention for signed-up free users stays <15% after 6 months of experiments, the product needs a bigger shift (e.g., shift to a different primary use case) rather than more activation tweaks.
